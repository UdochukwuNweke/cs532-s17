JSU picks up 3 more football commits

Tyler Cleveland , The Clarion-Ledger Published 7:09 p.m. CT Jan. 29, 2017 | Updated 7:29 p.m. CT Jan. 29, 2017
Buy Photo

Jackson State coach Tony Hughes hopes to round out his second recruiting class before National Signing Day on Wednesday.(Photo: Charles A. Smith/JSU Communications)Buy Photo

120 CONNECTTWEETLINKEDINCOMMENTEMAILMORE

Tony Hughes and the JSU football staff had relatively quiet weekend headed into the final stretch before national signing day on Wednesday.

The Tigers picked up three commitments — a pair of tight ends and an outside linebacker/defensive end — out of the seven prospects they hosted on campus for official visits this weekend.

It wasn’t as flashy as the past two weekends, when the staff hosted nearly 40 prospects and received pledges from 13 players, but the commitments they received Sunday did help address the team’s needs.

The two tight ends are Laquantis Hennington (6-6, 240), a senior from Bishop Dunn in Grand Prairie, Texas, and local prospect Robert Washington from Wingfield. Washington is a 6-foot-6, 230-pound basketball star who reportedly ran a 4.7-second 40 at a camp late last year.

Tyler Casby (6-2, 235) is a defensive end from Edna Karr in New Orleans. He started all 14 games for the state-champion Cougars who went undefeated.

The Tigers lost one commitment Sunday when West Jefferson (New Orleans) tight end Damien Degruy flipped to Fresno State. That likely opened the door for Washington, who had long been on JSU’s radar but hadn’t garnered an offer until this weekend.

The commitments bring Jackson State’s total to 23 with little more than 48 hours until national signing day.

Including the early enrollees, Hughes and his staff have nearly secured enough commitments to field a starting 22 made up entirely of freshmen — something he had stated was a goal entering his second recruiting process as a head coach.

As of Sunday, the Tigers have enrolled or received commitments from 16 offensive prospects: two quarterbacks, three running backs, five wide receivers, three tight ends and three offensive linemen.

On defense, they have recruited 12 players – five defensive linemen, two linebackers and five defensive backs.

If all 23 of the high school seniors currently committed stay with JSU, Hughes can take seven more players in the current class.
