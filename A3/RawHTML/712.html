Ellis, Watson top world-class indoor track athletes

James Johnson , @jjdandc Published 7:11 p.m. ET Jan. 28, 2017 | Updated 7:40 p.m. ET Jan. 28, 2017
Buy Photo

Erica Ellis was the state indoor pole vault champion last year at 12 feet, 3 inches. She cleared 12-7 this year to set a Section V record. The height is also the best ever for a sophomore in state history.(Photo: KATE MELTON)Buy Photo

Gates Chili sophomore Erica Ellis made her debut in world-class indoor pole vault competition Saturday in Boston, and ended up with a higher finish than a mentor, Jenn Suhr.

Ellis came in sixth in a field of seven at the New Balance Indoor Grand Prix with a height of 12 feet, 3/4 inch, inside the Reggie Lewis Center. This was the first time Ellis jumped during a competition at this level, as the meet opened the International Association of Athletics Federation (IAAF) World Indoor Tour. Five Olympians competed along with Ellis, one of two invited high school-aged jumpers.

Buy Photo

Sammy Watson (Photo: KATE MELTON)

Rio Olympic gold-medalist Katerina Stefanidi, of Greece, cleared 15-2 1/4 during her first-place finish. Suhr, a 34-year old Olympic champion who makes her home locally in Riga, no-heighted. Ellis set the Section V record at 12-7 Jan. 12 at The College at Brockport. She has trained with Jenn and coach Rick Suhr for more than a year.

Sammy Watson placed fourth in a field of eight, which included runners recognized in world rankings, during the women's 800-meters. Watson finished the two-lap race in 2:03.94, which may be another Section V record for the Rush-Henrietta senior who advanced to the USA Track and Field Olympic Trials last summer.

 

Buy Photo

Churchville-Chili senior Anna Kostarellis (Photo: KATE MELTON/PHOTOGRAPHER)

Charlene Lipsey, who attended high school in Nassau County on Long Island, won the women's 800 in 2:02.01, followed by Habitam Alemu, of Ethiopia.

Watson set the national high school girls indoor 600 record last week during a Section V meet in Houghton, and also owns the sectional indoor 400, 500 and 1-mile records.

Churchville-Chili senior Anna Kostarellis raced with other high school standouts and placed third in the junior girls mile, behind Jacqueline Gaughan (4:52.57), of New Hampshire.

Kostarellis, a three-time Democrat and Chronicle All-Greater Rochester selection by area high school coaches, finished in 4:55.52. Watson's Section V record is 4:47.49.
