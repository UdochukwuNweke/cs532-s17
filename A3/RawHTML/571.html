His first half puts him in the discussion for Most Improved Player, but Turner has seen a slight downward trend. He's seen his scoring, blocks and 3-point averages drop per game for each month from December to February. Myles Turner was quiet vs. the Wizards on Friday, scoring 10 points on 4-of-9 shooting.
The Wizards are a top-10 team in terms of points allowed to opposing centers, so give Marcin Gortat credit for slowing Turner down. He logged 31 minutes and hit both of his free throws, adding seven rebounds, three assists and one block in 31 minutes. Expect him to bounce back in a much better matchup on Saturday vs. the Bucks. Cleveland gives up the sixth-fewest points to opposing centers, as well as the eighth-fewest blocks, so the matchup worked against Turner tonight. This was his highest-scoring game in February, so hopefully it will jump-start him offensively. He's in the mix for Most Improved Player honors and has been a steady second-round fantasy value all season. Feb 8 - 10:11 PM
