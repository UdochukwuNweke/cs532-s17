Jeff Rutter opened his postgame press conference with a 66-second summation of Drakes 71-63 loss to Northern Iowa on Saturday. He touched on the Panthers stout defense andthe Bulldogs inability to hit timely shots, among other things, as the games deciding factors.

Shortly after his opening statement, Rutter was asked about his commitment to the long ball even after going 6 of 29 on Saturday. He didnt hesitate.

Absolutely, Rutter said. Were very intentional about getting clean 3-point shots. We believe thats a strength of our team, and well continue that throughout the season.

Drakes mark of 20.7 percent from deep against the Panthers was its second-lowest 3-point shooting performance of the season, trailing only the 3-for-15 showing against UMKC in mid-November. It was a stark step back from how the Bulldogs previously shot from deep especially at home.

The Bulldogs entered Saturdays matchup with five straight victories at the Knapp Center, during which they morphed into one of the Missouri Valley Conferences best shooting teams. In those five victories, Drake shot 45.7 percent from beyond the arc.

But during last Tuesdays game at Missouri State, Drake shot just 9 of 32 from deep, marking its lowest 3-point shooting performance since it went 10 of 36 against South Dakota State on Dec. 19. Those struggles carried over into Saturdays contest.

GAME RECAP: Northern Iowa tops Drake at the Knapp Center

Against the Panthers, Drake missed its first six 3-point shots, a skid that ended when Graham Woodward drilled one after 9 minutes and 22 seconds had passed. The Bulldogs went on to miss another six in a row, finishing the half down 25-22 and 1 of 13 from deep.

Part of this was Drakes inability to knock down open looks, Rutter said, but another reason was Northern Iowas stout defense. The Panthers allow just 66 points per game, good for fourth in the Valley and 52nd nationally.

Everything comes down to our defense, Northern Iowa junior forward Klint Carlson said. We started this year with one of the better defensive teams that I think Ive ever been a part of. Weve been holding teams to low scores.

At the beginning of the second half, they went on a little bit of a run, and we just had to buckle down and stick to what weve been doing. We just had to get stops on defense.

Indeed, Drake stormed back to score on four of its first five possessions in the second half, punctuated by a triple by DeAntae McMurray the teams second of the game to that point to tie the contest at 31.

But, like Carlson said, the Panthers buckled down, forcing the Bulldogs to miss their next five 3-point tries. It waspart of a stretch where they missed nine consecutive shots while Northern Iowa surged ahead for good.

    They communicated really well, said Ore Arogundade, who broke the string of nine straight misses in the second half with a triple. He would finish 3-for-6 from deep, making up half of Drakes made 3-pointers on Saturday.

    They were in the right spot at the right time always. Very well coached. They just executed really well, and that made it hard to find chances to score.

    MORE DRAKE COVERAGE: Hoops Therapy: Inside Ore Arogundade's rise at Drake

    Despite Northern Iowas tough defense, Rutter believes there are things his team can do better to improve their chances at creating open looks. Part of that is patience, but even then, he said, they still have to knock down the shots.

    To start the second half, we adjusted offensively and tried to go to a spread attack with more ball and player movement, Rutter said. Whats tempting with that is, even after two passes, we might feel like we have something good. Thats the game of basketball right there.

    Its like a kid walking around the candy shop, and you want to get the first piece of candy that you see, but if you walk around a little bit longer, you might find something better. Again, we want to be aggressive. We want to be on the attack. We want our opponent to be on their heels.

    We think were a good shooting team. Didnt shoot as well today. Its a setback, but its not going to make or break our season by any means.
